Mental Health Awareness The Anxiety and Depression Association of America cites anxiety as the most prevalent mental disorder in the U.S. Many people who suffer from anxiety have diagnosable anxiety disorders, but panic attacks and other symptoms of anxiety can affect otherwise mentally healthy people during situations of extreme stress.

Overcoming Anxiety and Panic

Remain Calm: This Anxiety is Nothing

What You Tell Yourself is Important

Practice rational self-statements and get rid of Automatic Negative Thoughts (ANTs). You can also try walking yourself through a meditation.

Surround Yourself with Calm

  1. Isochronic tones and binaural beats are ambient sounds with specific frequencies/bpms that are meant to coax your brain rhythms into syncing up and inducing different energy states. Studies have shown they can help with relaxation, meditation, and sleep.
  2. Imagine you are sitting in a quiet cafe, listening to the rain
  3. Browse relaxing images.
  4. Take a moment to play a calming game.

Take Your Zen with You

Mobile apps can be effective tools that make therapy more accessible, efficient, and portable. These self-help apps are not intended to be a substitute for treatment.

  • What's Up? A free app utilizing CBT (cognitive-behavioral therapy) and ACT (acceptance commitment therapy) methods. Useful features: negative thinking patterns tracker, diary, habit tracker, catastrophe scale.
  • Panic Relief This app (basic free and paid options) has easy-to-use coping tools to better manage and move through panic attacks. Skills taught: square breathing, diaphragmatic breathing, and progressive muscle relaxation.
  • Headspace A user-friendly app for meditation novices to apply meditative tools to reduce anxiety and improve awareness. Pros: Presents meditation in a user-friendly way with informative videos, gives access to an online forum; supplies podcasts, and normalizes mind-wandering.
